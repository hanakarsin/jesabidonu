Relief as 14-Year-Old Girl Abducted in Nyeri Is Rescued in Tanzania

  • The minor, who was kidnapped on August 25, 2021, was rescued at Mto wa Mbu town in Tanzania
  • DCI detectives arrested the main suspect, identified as Alex Jonathan Malel
  • Before the girl's disappearance, DCI said Malel, aged 19, worked as a night guard at an establishment near the minor's home

The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) detectives have rescued a 14-year-old girl who was abducted from her parents' home in Nyeri county.

The juvenile, who was kidnapped on August 25, 2021, was rescued at Mto wa Mbu town in Tanzania, approximately 514-kilometers away from her parents' home.

Taking to Twitter on Monday, September 27, the investigations agency said they had arrested the main suspect, identified as Alex Jonathan Malel.

Before the girl's disappearance, DCI said Malel, aged 19, worked as a night guard at an establishment near the minor's home.

The class eight pupil was on her mid-term break when the unfortunate incident happened.

On the fateful night, she had stepped out of their house to answer the call of nature at 2am, only for her parents to wake up in the morning and find their daughter missing.

They immediately reported the matter at Gatitu police post but due to the gravity of the matter, Nyeri county criminal investigations officer John Gacheru took over the investigations.

In a well-planned rescue mission by DCI's elite Special Service Unit and Tanzania's regional crime office based in Arusha, Malel was finally cornered within Engarika area and the girl was rescued.

Tears flowed freely from the minor's relatives and friends, as she was reunited with her distraught kin who have known no peace since her disappearance.

Malel was detained at Mto wa Mbu police station where he is expected to be charged with sexual assault.
