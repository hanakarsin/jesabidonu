Cavina School: admission, fees, curriculum, owner, contacts

Cavina School is among international schools that continue to take the spotlight in the Kenyan market. As the appetite for quality education continues to grow, Kenyans are increasingly embracing the international curriculum. Many parents are eager to enrol their kids in top schools other than those offering Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) in a bid to provide immeasurable opportunities for their young ones.

Cavina is a British Preparatory School located in Nairobi. It admits students from all nationalities from ages three to thirteen. The school has carved a targeted niche market, and its name remains superior in the education sector.

Cavina School Managing Director

Cavina was founded in 1950 by H Massie-Blomfield, a renowned Kenyan-British educationist. The school started in his compound as a boy’s school, but it has expanded its doors to accommodate over 200 students. Cavina school owner Mr. Blomfield is also the school’s headmaster.

About Cavina School

The academic establishment is located along Elgeyo Marakwet Road, off Argwins Kodhek Road in Kilimani. Cavina school reviews are impressive, with a 4.7star rating. Admissions for new students are made in September when the international curriculum school year begins.

To earn a spot in this prestigious school, a potential student must be the right age for the class before enrolment. The school takes children from all faiths. However, they must all attend morning chapel devotions.

Admissions at Cavina

Due to its mantra in raising an upright moulded child, Cavina’s admissions are somewhat competitive. With a maximum of 20 students per class, there is a learner-centred focus, which means the teacher does not get overwhelmed.

Similarly, control and quality services are guaranteed. Parents must make an early booking to enroll their children at Cavina School.

Students aged five and above are required to write a brief personal statement before registration. Details for the Cavina School Kenya fee structure are not published, and interested stakeholders must personally visit the school to determine the cost.

Cavina School learning approach

Cavina focuses on students' innovativeness, all-roundedness, and out-of-the-box learning. Education for children at tender ages centres on play rather than orthodox classroom learning.

Students are introduced to extra formal learning in their later years while promoting research and creativity. Extra-curricular activities are encouraged and considered an essential aspect of academic excellence.

The school is big on building a Christian foundation in its learners. Therefore, its management is keen to strengthen learners' character through discipline and not purely on a reasonable basis. This instills empathy, self-drive and good manners in the children.

Upon completing their studies, students in their final years sit for a series of exams. When they pass, they get enrolled in local and international high schools for secondary education.
