George Muchai: Taxi Driver Hired by Killers of Ex-Kabete MP Recounts Chilling Events 6 Years Later

  • At around 3.30am on Saturday, February 7, 2015, Morris Njuguna received a call from one of his clients asking him to pick him up at Kangemi
  • Njuguna, a then taxi driver, would later realise that the client and three more men whom he carried on that dawn were involved in the murder of ex-MP George Muchai
  • He provided crucial leads during the investigations but was later struck off the witness protection list and he now spends every waking moment looking over his shoulder

On the wee of Saturday, February 7, 2015, Kabete MP George Muchai, his two bodyguards and a driver were shot dead at the Nairobi Central Business District.

A taxi driver who was hired by the assassins after the murders and who also became a key witness in the investigation still replays the events that occurred on the fateful dawn and which changed his life forever.

Speaking to TV47, Morris Njuguna recalled that at around 3.30am on that day, he received a call from one of his clients whom he had carried a number of times.

The client told Njuguna to pick him up at the Total Kangemi Service Station. As soon as he picked him up in his taxi, they both began their journey heading in the Kikuyu direction.

"After arriving near the Kabete Police Station, he removed a pistol from his back and I immediately asked him what was going on but he told me not to worry about anything," he said.

Upon arrival at Muguga, the client asked him to stop and pick another man who was masked and who was later placed at the centre of the crime by an eyewitness who testified during the court proceedings.

For the cab driver, he thought the men were from a robbery spree but it would later dawn on him that they were prime suspects in the homicides of the MP and his aides.

"This guy who got into the vehicle with a hoodie on, he started asking how the crime scene had been left and if any finger prints had been left," Njuguna recalled.

Three more men would later board the taxi and it took an about-turn heading back to the Nairobi direction. On the way, the gang disposed off the murder weapons.

They later alighted at Mountain View and Kangemi. A few days later, he pieced together the pieces of the puzzle and figured the men he had carried orchestrated Muchai's murder.

He thus decided to talk to a senior police officer who was his friend and he even took the law enforcers to the places where the murder weapons were disposed.

He was later kept under the witness protection program but after he testified in court, the government abandoned him.

"They had promised to change my identify so that I could be safer and they had also promised to relocate me," he said adding none of the promises had been kept.

Six years on, Njuguna still lives in fear and he lays in hope that someday, the government will keep its promises so that he can get his life back.
