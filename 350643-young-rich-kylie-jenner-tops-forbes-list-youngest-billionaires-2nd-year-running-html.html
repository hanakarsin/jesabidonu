Young rich: Kylie Jenner tops Forbes list of youngest self-made billionaires 2nd year running

- Businesswoman Kylie Jenner maintained her status as the youngest and richest self-made personality in the world for the second year in a row

- The reality TV star sold 51% shares of her company, Kylie Cosmetics, to beauty giant Coty Inc. for KSh 63 billion

- The 22-year-old is believed to be worth KSh 120 billion after the sale

- Kylie also made the list of 10 billionaires in the world under the age of 30

Forbes Magazine has named make-up mogul Kylie Jenner the youngest self-made billionaire in the world for the second year in a row.

Kylie took the crown in 2019 after knocking Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg off the throne, who made his first billion at the age of 23.

The reality TV star sold 51% shares of her company, Kylie Cosmetics, to beauty giant Coty Inc. for KSh 63 billion, making her total amount rise to KSh 120 billion.

According to Forbes, the deal closed in January 2020, with the remaining shares enabling her join the list of 2,095 people in the world with a ten-digit fortune.

She also made the list of 10 billionaires in the world under the age of 30.

Previously, Forbes was forced to address backlash for calling Kylie self-made, as people gave the opinion that she came from a well off family.

The magazine clarified the young queen had not inherited her business and she built it from scratch.

Kylie is also one of the few people to gave largely to the coronavirus fight with a huge KSh 100 million donation.

The mother of one said she hoped the money would help provide doctors with the required gear and other essentials needed.
