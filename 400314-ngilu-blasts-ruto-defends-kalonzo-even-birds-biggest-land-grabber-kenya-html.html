Ngilu blasts Ruto, defends Kalonzo: &quot;Even birds know who the biggest land grabber in Kenya is&quot;

- Deputy President William Ruto had claimed Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka's Yatta home sits on a grabbed government land

- Kitui governor Charity Ngilu defended Kalonzo saying the land was genuinely acquired by the former vice president

- She claimed Ruto was the most notorious land grabber in the country who gave her a lot of problems during her tenure as the Lands CS

Kitui governor Charity Ngilu has come out boldly to defend Wiper Democratic Movement Party leader Kalonzo Musyoka from the accusations of land grabbing levelled against him by Deputy President William Ruto.

Ngilu, in an uncharacteristic defense of her longtime political foe, dismissed Ruto's allegations as frivolous and desperate attempts to disparage the reputation of other leaders.

Genuine transaction

The Kitui county boss said she had the opportunity to probe how Kalonzo acquired the land in question while serving as the Lands Cabinet Secretary and was satisfied with the manner in which the former vice president became its owner.

Speaking to journalists at Manyenyoni in Kitui county where she toured the Kitui Youth Enterprise Centre (KIYOSEC), Ngilu said Ruto was the most notorious land grabber in the country and admitted to having a lot of problems with him while serving as the Lands CS.

"Trying to link him (Kalonzo) to land grabbing is ill-conceived because each and every Kenyan, even birds of the air know who the biggest land grabber in this country is,” she said.

Langata Primary School land

The governor accused the second in command of attempting to grab the Langata Primary Schools land "were it not for the intervention of the president".

Ngilu alleged she advised the DP several times to leave the piece of land for pupils to use as their playing ground but her advice fell on deaf ears.

"I saw the worst of Ruto while serving as CS for Lands, attempting to grab land belonging to poor children from Kibera slums knowing very well they do not have any other playground was too bad,” said Ngilu.

"Dangerous man"

The governor took cue from Kalonzo who had earlier called out Ruto for trying to hoodwink Kenyans into believing he was their saviour when he was actually "a dangerous man".

The Wiper leader linked the deputy president to about nine scandals which he noted define his legacy.

Key among them was the alleged irregular accusation of a piece of land on which Weston Hotel sits, alleged grabbing of the land belonging to Hon. Joseph Murumbi, Kenya’s first Vice-President, suspicious acquisition of 100 acres belonging to the late Adrian Muteshi, Arror and Kimwaer dams scandal among others.

"I challenge DP Ruto alias 'Arap Mashamba' well known for his greed for both public and private land, to offer himself for investigation," Kalonzo said.

"Kazi bila Wizi"

During his public address in Bomet about a week ago, Ruto claimed Kalonzo grabbed the Yatta land and set up his official residence.

The DP was responding to the Wiper leader's accusations that he was a corrupt leader whose newly formed United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party should consider changing its slogan from Kazi ni Kazi to Kazi ni Kazi bila Wizi (sic).

"You have been in leadership for 40 years without doing anything and your only record is that you are living on government land which you have turned to be yours," said Ruto.
