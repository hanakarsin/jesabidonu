Uhuru takes over as new chairperson of East African Community

- Uhuru on Saturday, February 27, joined other regional leaders for a virtual meeting of the 21st ordinary summit of the East African Community heads of state

- Rwandan President Paul Kagame was appointed EAC chairperson in 2019

- Kenya's Peter Mutuku Mathuki and Justice Kathurima M'Inoti were also sworn-in as secretary-general the EAC and Judge of the East African Court of Justice, respectively

President Uhuru Kenyatta has replaced Rwandan President Paul Kagame as the new chairperson of the East African Community (EAC).

State House announced in a statement on Saturday, February 27.

The house on the hill also said Kenya's Peter Mutuku Mathuki and Justice Kathurima M'Inoti were sworn-in as secretary-general the EAC and Judge of the East African Court of Justice, respectively.

Uhuru had on Saturday morning joined other regional leaders for a virtual meeting of the 21st ordinary summit of the East African Community heads of state.

The president vowed to take the region to new heights as he thanked the community for showing faith in him.

"Thank you for showing confidence, faith and trust in me. I promise to do all I can to take our region to greater heights."

Uhuru's appointment stirred mixed reactions from Kenyans, with a section hailing him while others said there was nothing to celebrate about.

TUKO.co.ke sampled some reactions:

Josephat Kipkorir wrote:

"Peace and unity is the only way to strengthen commercial ties between countries. Congratulations, Mr President, for ensuring we are at peace with the neighbouring countries and your new position."

Abdallah Kamanja said:

" Congratulations Mr President. Kenyan's are happy because of the handshake. You are a good leader who deserves a reward."

The EAC virtual summit was attended by presidents Salva Kiir (South Sudan), Paul Kagame (Rwanda), Yoweri Museveni (Uganda) and Évariste Ndayishimiye (Burundi), while Tanzania's John Magufuli sent a representative.

Kagame had been appointed EAC chairperson in 2019.

The East African Comunity's heads of states meet annually and set key priorities at the highest, regional political level. They also discuss business submitted to it by the Council of Ministers.
