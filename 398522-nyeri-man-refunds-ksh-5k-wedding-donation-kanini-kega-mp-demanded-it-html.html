Nyeri: Man refunds KSh 5k wedding donation from Kanini Kega after MP demanded it

- MP Kanini Kega gave Zack Kihenjo KSh 5,000 during his wedding in December 2019

- However, a couple of days ago, Kihenjo questioned Kega's development record and this did not sit right with the MP

- The legislator through his personal assistant demanded his donation back; the man's friends helped him raise the funds and they sent it to the lawmaker

A man from Nyeri has refunded the KSh 5,000 which Kieni MP Kanini Kega donated during his wedding after the lawmaker demanded the money back when he criticised him.

Zack Maina Kihenjo said the MP jointly contributed KSh 10,000 with his Constituency Development Fund (CDF) chairman Maina Munene during his wedding in December 2019.

Speaking to the media, Kihenjo said the MP and CDF official each gave him KSh 5,000 for the wedding.

However, recently, the Nyeri man questioned Kega's development record in the constituency and this did not please the Kieleweke parliamentarian who seemingly thought Kihenjo had no right to check him since he had helped him a little less than a year ago.

"Recently, I asked a question about development in Kieni but no one responded to me. However, yesterday, I woke to a message demanding that I refund all the money that I was given for my wedding," he said.

Kihenjo shared the predicament with his friends who advised him to pay up the money or end up being a slave to the parliamentarian.

His pals helped him raise the money and after hitting the KSh 10,000 target, he sent KSh 5,00 the MP and the rest to the CDF chair.

One of the residents condemned Kega for his behaviour and told him if he wanted refunds from all the fundraisers he had attended, they also were ready to give the money back.

"Even for the other harambees that he has attended, if he wants the money, we will still refund it," one local said."We decided to contribute the money because we did not want a situation where the two of them are embarrassing our friend every day," another man said.

In a separate story, a number of tenants in Nairobi's Kahawa Wendani estate called out the Kieni MP for allegedly locking them out over rent default.

The tenants at Joshua Acarde resorted to locking the apartment's caretaker's house accusing him of having harassed them.

The aggrieved tenants recorded a video and shared it on various social media platforms to voice their grievances adding that power had also been disconnected from the houses.
