Majority of Kenyans Ask Raila Not to Contest in 2022 Elections, TUKO.co.ke Survey

  • In the survey, 45.5% of the respondents felt Raila Odinga should not contest for the top seat again
  • However, 43.6% said the seasoned politician should give his fifth stab at the presidency
  • 7.3% of the polled said Raila should work with Deputy President William Ruto who is also eyeing the same seat

The majority of Kenyans have asked the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga not to contest for the presidency in the 2022 General Election.

Initially, the Opposition leader had said he would join the race to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta if Kenyans asked him to.

A poll conducted and compiled by TUKO.co.ke on Saturday, August 28, showed that 45.5% of the respondents felt Raila should not contest for the top seat again.

Of those polled, 43.6% said the seasoned politician should give his fifth stab at the presidency in polls slated for Tuesday, August 9, 2022.

Reactions

Responding to the question on whether Raila should vie again, here is what some of them had to say:

"I voted him several times loosing.Lastly he joined Uhuru by being cheated.So,I've realized he has no focus.Let him rest peacefully."

"Yes, Raila is the best bet for this country. Stands for good governance."

Amina Dibo said:

"No. He completely let us down. He had strong fans but the moment he killed the opposition we also quitted. He's not sincere. Too late,take a rest."

Jackson Chege said:

"Baba is a true hero above all a legend, he fought a good fight for these reasons I see Bondo as the perfect destination for Baba to rest."

Fighting corruption

In other news, a majority of Kenyans said leaders in leading political parties have no interest in ending corruption in Kenya but are rather interesting in pushing for their own selfish agendas.

In a poll carried out by TUKO.co.ke on its social media platforms, many expressed their scepticism on political big wigs' commitment to the fight against corruption.

Responding to the question on whether leaders in Raila Odinga's ODM, Deputy President William Ruto's United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party, One Kenya Alliance and Jubilee Party are best-placed to fight graft, many Kenyans said they do not have confidence in them.
