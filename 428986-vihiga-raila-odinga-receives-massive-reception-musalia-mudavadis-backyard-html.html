Vihiga: Raila Odinga Receives Massive Reception in Musalia Mudavadi's Backyard

  • Raila was in Mudavadi's Vihiga home turf on Friday, October 8, to woo new voters encouraging residents to register ahead of the 2022 polls
  • The former prime minister's first stopover was at Khiwliba Secondary School in Luanda constituency
  • leaders in Raila's entourage included; Governors Wycliffe Oparanya (Kakamega), Wilber Ottichilo (Vihiga), among others

Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga received massive reception in his Amani National Congress (ANC) counterpart, Musalia Mudavadi's Vihiga backyard.

Raila was in Mudavadi's home turf on Friday, October 8, to woo new voters encouraging residents to register during the 30-day Enhanced Continuous Voter Registration drive.

''I was impressed by the progress of the ongoing nationwide voter registration exercise. I am calling on our people to stand up and be counted. Step forward and register,'' Raila said.

Raila drummed up support for his 2022 presidential bid

The former prime minister also used the opportunity to drum up support for his 2022 presidential bid.

''I publicly said that I would make a major announcement at the end of this month and I now want to see that my foot soldiers are well armed. Make sure that you have the gun (national identity card) and bullet (vote),'' Raila told residents.

Raila said that his decision to be on the ballot box as a presidential candidate in 2022 would depend on the number of new voters registered in his political strongholds across the country after the ongoing national voter registration exercise ends in early November.

''I am going to monitor and see how you my people would have enlisted as voters. If you register in large numbers then I will announce that I am ready to go for the country's top seat,'' Raila explained.

Mulembe nation urged to rally behind Raila

Leaders present urged the Mulembe nation to support Raila's presidential bid claiming that he is the only one who can make sure that the community gets the presidency once he retires.

''In western Kenya region, we are looking at 1.2 million new voters which will translate to Raila's votes,'' said ODM secretary-general Edwin Sifuna.

Raila insisted that Kenyans have only one choice in 2022 to vote out corrupt leaders using corrupt money for their political campaigns.

''We know some of them who go to churches and donate vehicles to members of the clergy. All we are saying that all these people don't deserve to hold public offices because their images are tainted by corrupt dealings,'' Raila further said.

Raila's foot soldiers

Even though Raila has not declared officially that he will be vying for the presidency in 2022, his foot soldiers seized the moment to call upon the Mulembe nation to bank their votes on him ahead of the polls.

''The most important thing in Raila's manifesto is that he will be able to put up factories across the western Kenya region that would in turn translate to job opportunities for our people,'' said Luanda MP Chris Omulele.

Other leaders in Raila's entourage included; Governors Wycliffe Oparanya (Kakamega), Wilber Ottichilo (Vihiga), MPs Caleb Hamisi (Saboti), Godfrey Osotsi (nominated) and former Funyula MP Paul Otuoma, among others.

The leaders dismissed other 2022 presidential hopefuls, including Mudavadi and Bungoma senator Moses Wetang'ula saying they were not ripe for the top seat.

Raila later held a rally at Luanda market with all eyes now being on the turn out of the voter registration exercise as the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) targets more than half a million voters from the region in addition to more than two million voters currently.
