Former Sports CS Echesa freed on KSh 1m cash bail in IEBC official assault case

- Police apprehended Echesa on Friday, March 5, after slapping an IEBC official during the chaotic March 4, Matungu by-election

- Kiambu Chief Magistrate Patricia Gichohi directed the case to be mentioned on April 9

- Echesa had insisted that his political enemies instigated his arrest

Former Sports Cabinet Secretary Rashid Echesa, who is accused of assaulting an Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) employee, has been released from custody.

Echesa was apprehended on Friday, March 5, after allegedly slapping an IEBC official during the chaotic March 4, Matungu by-election.

The defiant politician was freed on a KSh 1 million cash bail or a KSh 1 million bond in a ruling by Kiambu Chief Magistrate Patricia Gichohi on Tuesday, March 9.

Gichohi directed the case to be mentioned on April 9.

Caught on camera

Echesa was captured in a video angrily lecturing an IEBC official, demanding to know why he had chased United Democratic Alliance (UDA) agents before removing his mask and slapping him.

The IEBC chairperson Wafula Chebukati had termed Echesa's action as an apparent attack on the law.

Denying charges

As previously reported, Echesa insisted that his arrest was instigated by his political enemies.

The youthful politician said the video of him assaulting the IEBC official was edited by his enemies to paint him as a violent person as part of their scheme to intimidate him.

Echesa further affirmed that his tribulations resulted from his political association with Deputy President William Ruto.

However, he insisted arresting him will not cow him from supporting the second in command's 2022 presidential bid.

"I was born in the ghetto and being thrown into a cell is nothing big, it is familiar territory. Throwing me in the cell will not cow me from my conviction that Ruto is better placed to become the president of our country in 2022,” he added.
