'People Are Pained That Men Dont Sponsor My Finances'

Former Big Brother Naija ‘PepperDem’ Housemate, Natacha Akide popularly known as Tacha, has taken to social media to brag about sponsoring her ‘baby girl lifestyle’, all by herself.

Naija News reports that the disqualified housemate in a post shared on the microblogging platform, Twitter, claimed that some people are pained that men aren’t sponsoring her finances.

According to Tacha, she will continue to make noise about her achievements because making money as a woman in today’s society is not easy.

The reality TV star said that she would continue to be an inspiration to young girls adding that a lady does not need men to succeed.

She tweeted: “The fact men don’t sponsor my finances dey to pain a lot of people!! Are you ANNOYED!!!? ANNOYED

“I will loud it!!! If e easy RUN AM!! I will loud loud loud it!!! As e dey pain you E dey sweet me.

“you think it’s easy as a woman to make legit money in this our society!!?? I laugh in “Don’tt Loud i.”
I will keep lauding it for all the young girls that LOOK UP TO ME!!! You do not need MEN TO MAKE IT!!! They just should be a bonus not a LADDER TO UPGRADE”

Lawmaker Warns Mercy Chinwo

Meanwhile, the lawmaker representing Afikpo North-East in Ebonyi State House of Assembly, Maria Ude Nwachi, has issued a warning to gospel singer, Mercy Chinwo and her husband Pastor Blessed over displaying their wealth online.

Recall that Naija News had earlier reported that Chinwo turned 31 on September 5th and her husband took to his Instagram page to pray for her and celebrate her new year.

A subsequent video also showed the moment he gifted her a luxury car to celebrate her birthday.

Many Nigerians had celebrated with the gospel singer on the gift from her husband.

However, reacting to the act via her Facebook account, the lawmaker chided Chinwo and her husband for their public display of affluence.

Nwachi insisted that such a display would draw a lot of curiosity which might eventually lead to an investigation.

She went on to state that public display of affluence is mostly seen amongst Nollywood stars and not her type.
