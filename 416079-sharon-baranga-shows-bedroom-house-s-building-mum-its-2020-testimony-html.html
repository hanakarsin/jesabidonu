Sharon Barang'a Shows House She's Building Parents: Dad Died Same Day I Bought Roofing&quot;

  • TV 47 editor Sharon Barang'a unveiled a four-bedroom bungalow she is building for her mum
  • The former NTV reporter disclosed that her dad died the same day she delivered roofing materials
  • She hailed the masons who worked around the clock to make the house ready for mourners

Media personality Sharon Barang’a has unveiled the house she is building upcountry for her parents.

Via a YouTube video on June 19, 2021, Barang’a said the self-contained house has four bedrooms.

“I started it as a joke, and I did not know I would hack it. But I like the final design,” she said.

According to the TV 47 education editor, the wiring and plumbing are done.

“80% of the house is done. I started building with savings and then applied for a loan to top it up,” the former NTV reporter said.

Tragedy strikes

However, tragedy struck on the same day the PhD holder reached an essential milestone in the construction.

According to Barang'a, her father died on the same day they delivered the roofing material.

She described her father’s passing as devastating, noting that he never lived in the house she toiled to put up for them.

“On the same day we were delivering the roofing material on June 5, 2020, my father died. So one of the things that really hurt is building the house for them, yet he will not live inside it. It was devastating,” she said.

Masons go the extra mile

Barang'a revealed that the masons worked day and night after his death to ensure the house was ready for mourners.

“The fundis outdid themselves by working day and night to make the was house befitting so mourners could stay there,” she said.

The media personality further pointed out that she is slowly working on the house and plans on completing it by the end of 2021.

“I have just started working on the interior,” Barang’a said.

House tour

In the 15-minute video, Barang’a gave a brief tour of the house with underground water storage tanks.

“The roof's construction allows for the harvesting of a lot of water,” said the journalist.

Girl child

On social Facebook, Barang’a expressed gratitude to God for enabling her to construct the house.

“I hope and pray this video inspires someone out there. Do not look down on the girl child. We can also come through for our people and society,” she concluded.

Losing job

Soon after her father's death, Barang'a lost her job at NTV in what the company called a reorganisation exercise to stay afloat during the pandemic.
