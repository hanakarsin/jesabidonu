Former Nairobi governor Mike Sonko has indicated he plans to run in the upcoming city by-election slated for Thursday, February 18.

According to a report by Capital News, the flamboyant politician said he had obtained Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA), Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI), Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) and Higher Education Loans Board (HELB) clearance certificates in his quest to defend his seat.

Sonko's move came after former Kiambu county boss Ferdinand Waititu got a reprieve after the Independent, Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) cleared him to compete in the Nairobi gubernatorial by-election despite facing corruption charges in court.

The electoral body on Wednesday, January 6, gazetted Waititu's name among 12 independent candidates who expressed their intention to replace Sonko whose impeachment by the Nairobi County Assembly was upheld by the Senate on December 18, 2020.

Legal experts say Sonko can't bounce back

Legal experts have however, warned Sonko cannot run for public office again after he was kicked out of office through impeachment over various charges including abuse of office.

Lawyer Steve Ogolla opined Sonko cannot bounce back in any elective position because he was found to have to have breached the law twice; first by the county assembly and later the Senate.

"No. Sonko cannot run again. If you look at Article 182 of the Constitution, the vacancy has arisen as a consequence of his removal under Article 181 of the Constitution. It is a consequence of gross misconduct and abuse of office," Ogolla said.

Former Senate deputy speaker Kithure Kindiki who is also the Tharaka Nithi senator on the other hand argued impeached county chiefs cannot subsequently run for election to the office of governor.

â€œIt appears from the wordings of the Constitution that a person charged in court but is yet to be convicted or one who has been convicted but has not exhausted the appeal avenues can be cleared to vie for political office, but not one who is removed from office whether by court or impeachment or any other way for breach of chapter six of the Constitution," Kindiki said.

Campaigns for the Nairobi race would officially commence on, Monday, January 18, and end on Monday, February 15.
