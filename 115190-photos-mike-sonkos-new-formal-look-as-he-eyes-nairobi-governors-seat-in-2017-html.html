Mike Sonko changes dress code as he eyes to unseat Kidero

- Nairobi gubernatorial aspirant Senator Mike Sonko is changing his dressing code in order to look the part. Sonko said he had to change his hairstyle, reduce number of jewels as he embraces a more formal look associated with the seat

- Sonko is set to fight for the Jubilee ticket against former devolution minister Anne Waiguru and several others before meeting Nairobi Governor Evans at the ballot 

Nairobi Senator Mike Sonko is gradually changing his dress code ahead of the 2017 gubernatorial race.

Mike Sonko, on Sunday, April 3, said he had been forced to drop some of his fashion choices and jewelry in order to look the part.

The senator said he had stopped dying his hair and is forced to keep the number of jewelry to the minimum.

He is also considering to donate the golden rings and necklaces as he no longer put them on in most occasions. Mike Sonko said the jewelry are mostly limited to weekends and at night.

In May 2014, Mike Sonko's dressing visibly amused President Uhuru and other top state officials. Mike Sonko had turned up at the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport to bid Chinese Prime Minister Li Keqiang farewell in a trendy slim fit ripped jeans.

A lot has clearly changed since then. Mike Sonko has since been dressing formally whenever he goes for official functions.

Mike Sonko would have to battle it out for the Jubilee Nairobi gubernatorial ticket with a number of interested politicians first before meeting Kidero at the ballot.

Former cabinet secretary Anne Waiguru had already declared her interest in the Jubilee ticket. Others said to be eyeing the Jubilee ticket to replace Nairobi Governor Evans Kidero are: Nominated MP Johnstone Sakaja, former Starehe MP Margaret Wanjiru, Dagoretti South MP Dennis Waweru and Harun Mwau.
