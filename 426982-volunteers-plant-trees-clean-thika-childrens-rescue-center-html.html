World Cleanup Day: Volunteers Clean Thika Childrens Rescue Centre, Plant Trees

  • World Cleanup Day is an annual global social action program aimed at combating the global solid waste problem
  • The Environment & Solid Waste Management Directorate marked the day at Thika sub-county
  • Volunteers led by Superfoam Ltd planted trees at the centre

On Saturday, September 18, several volunteers helped clean up the Thika Children's Rescue Centre during World Cleanup Day.

The event is an annual global social action program aimed at combating the global solid waste problem.

The Environment & Solid Waste Management Directorate marked the day at Thika sub-county by cleaning up the Thika children's Rescue Centre and its surroundings. They also planted many samples around the area.

Various companies and educational institutions from Kiambu county participated in the clean-up drive.

Superfoam Ltd, a mattress manufacturing company based in Ruiru, was at the frontline at the event. The staff members actively engaged in the cleaning and tree planting exercise.

Superfoam Ltd, head of marketing, Sanskrati Shetty, said:

"We are more than grateful to the Kiambu county government for inviting us to be part of this exercise. We, as a brand, pride ourselves in safeguarding our surrounding environment by taking the necessary conservation measures. "We encourage the county government to spearhead such cleaning and conservation activities within the county and extend our support to all their initiatives."

Director, environment and solid waste management, Patrick Theuri, the chief guest, noted that domestic waste generators committed illegality by not engaging private garbage collectors to manage their waste.

Also present were Monica Kingori, assistant director environment & solid waste management, and county forester Simon Chege.

Superfoam Limited continues to show endless support to projects in the surrounding community. For example, they recently made a massive donation to Mukuyu Primary School during the school's Prize Giving Day Celebrations.

In June, children left under the care of police officers at the Ruiru Police Station were a happy lot after Superfoam Lt donated several mattresses to ease their sleep arrangements.

Infants and other young children are left at the station by estranged parents often, and the officers shelter them in the police station until they find a home for them.

The OCS- Ruiru Police Station, Samuel Mwangi said it was usually very traumatizing for these children, and the COVID-19 pandemic made the situation even worse.
